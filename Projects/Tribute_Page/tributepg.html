Mother Teresa

Guardian Angel Who Touched Countless Lives

Here's a Timeline of Mother Teresa:

  • 1910 - Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu, later known as Mother Teresa, is born in Skopje, Ottoman Empire (modern-day North Macedonia).
  • 1928 - Agnes leaves home to join the Sisters of Loreto in Ireland, where she takes the name Sister Mary Teresa.
  • 1929 - She arrives in India and begins her novitiate in Darjeeling
  • 1931 - Sister Teresa takes her first vows, and she is assigned to teach at St. Mary's High School in Calcutta.
  • 1937 - Sister Teresa takes her final vows and becomes Mother Teresa.
  • 1946 - Mother Teresa experiences what she describes as the "call within a call" during a train journey to Darjeeling. She feels a divine urging to serve the poorest of the poor.
  • 1948 - After receiving permission from the Vatican and the local archbishop, Mother Teresa starts her own religious order, the Missionaries of Charity.
  • 1950 - The Missionaries of Charity officially begins its work with Mother Teresa's first disciple and the establishment of a small school in the slums of Calcutta.
  • 1952 - Mother Teresa opens Nirmal Hriday, the first Home for the Dying, providing care and dignity to those in their final days.
  • 1956 - The Missionaries of Charity expands its work outside of India, opening a house in Venezuela.
  • 1971 - Mother Teresa receives the Pope John XXIII Peace Prize for her dedication to serving the poor and promoting peace.
  • 1979 - Mother Teresa is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for her humanitarian work.
  • 1980 - She receives the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.
  • 1987 - Mother Teresa passes away in Calcutta at the age of 87.
  • 2005 - Mother Teresa is canonized as a saint by Pope Francis in a ceremony at the Vatican.
